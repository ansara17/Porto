Case 01

School Data System Optimization

Context

School operations required accurate student data that could be regularly updated for administrative work, reporting, synchronization, and coordination with relevant stakeholders. The scope covered approximately 120–130 active student records.

Problem
  • Data and documents were stored across several files with structures that were not always consistent.
  • Legacy records required correction and validation before they could be used reliably.
  • Document retrieval and data review took longer when storage structures and naming conventions were inconsistent.
  • The process needed to remain understandable for other users and the next operator.
Actions
  • Created a digital folder hierarchy based on document types and operational requirements.
  • Established file naming patterns and templates to make documents easier to locate.
  • Managed data entry, correction, validation, synchronization, and maintenance through Dapodik.
  • Cleaned legacy data and corrected inconsistent information.
  • Documented the workflow so the process could be continued by other users.
Tools
Dapodik Microsoft Excel Digital Filing Data Validation Document Control
Results
  • Data and documents became more structured and easier to retrieve.
  • The risk of inconsistency was reduced through routine validation and correction.
  • Several retrieval, review, and administrative processes could be completed on the same day or within hours.
  • Data coordination with teachers, staff, parents, other operators, and education authorities became more organized.
Handover

The folder structure, templates, and process documentation were organized so they could be understood and used by teachers, staff, and the next operator after the role transition.

Case 02

Financial Administration Workflow

Context

School financial administration required consistency across budget planning, bookkeeping, realization recording, period closing, and reporting through the ARKAS system.

Problem
  • Each stage required accuracy and disciplined documentation.
  • Differences between budget data, bookkeeping, and realization records could delay reporting.
  • Supporting documents needed to be organized so they could be reviewed efficiently.
  • The operational cycle needed to be completed according to reporting periods and school requirements.
Actions
  • Managed budget input and adjustments through ARKAS.
  • Maintained bookkeeping records and the BKU general cash book.
  • Tracked realization and reviewed consistency between budget and expenditure data.
  • Handled period-closing processes and prepared supporting information for reporting.
  • Organized financial documents and records into a structure that was easier to trace.
Tools
ARKAS Budgeting Bookkeeping BKU Reporting Support
Results
  • The financial administration cycle became more structured from budgeting through reporting.
  • Budget, bookkeeping, and realization records became easier to review.
  • Supporting documentation became more organized and traceable by reporting period.
  • Reporting preparation was supported by more consistent and documented information.
Handover

The document structure and workflow were organized so the financial administration process could be continued by the next operator or staff member using clearer references.

Microsoft Excel Microsoft Word Document Control Reporting Coordination

  • Management received more organized operational information.
Handover

Records and supporting documents were maintained in organized physical and digital structures to support continued use by related departments.

Case 04

Shipment Records and Operational Coordination

Context

Daily branch operations required accurate records of incoming and outgoing shipments, recipient information, delivery status, and supporting documents.

Problem
  • Shipment data needed to match physical and operational records.
  • Status discrepancies required timely follow-up.
  • Couriers and branch staff depended on accurate information.
  • Daily summaries needed to be prepared consistently.
Actions
  • Entered and reviewed tracking numbers, recipient details, destinations, and shipment statuses.
  • Verified supporting documents and delivery records.
  • Prepared branch operational summaries.
  • Coordinated discrepancies with couriers and operations staff.
  • Assisted customers with basic shipment information.
Tools
Shipment Records Data Entry Verification Daily Reporting Customer Support
Results
  • Shipment records remained more organized and traceable.
  • Data discrepancies could be followed up with the relevant operational staff.
  • Daily reporting was supported by clearer information.
  • Customers received more consistent basic shipment information.
Handover

Shipment documents and branch records were maintained according to the operational filing process so they remained available for subsequent shifts and follow-up.

Operational Improvement Principles

Principles I apply when organizing processes and systems.

Improvement does not always require a complex system. Structure, consistency, documentation, and selecting appropriate tools often create the most practical operational impact.

01

Structure before automation

Data, documents, and workflows should be organized before digital tools or automation are introduced.

02

Standardization reduces repetitive work

Templates, naming patterns, and consistent workflows help reduce repetition and formatting differences.

03

Centralized access supports collaboration

Data that is easier to locate and access helps users work without relying heavily on manual file exchange.

04

Documentation supports continuity

A useful system should be understandable and maintainable by the next user, not only by its original creator.

05

Use tools that match the requirement

A lightweight solution is often more appropriate when it resolves the bottleneck without creating unnecessary complexity.

06

Accuracy remains a priority

Faster processes should still include validation, correction, document control, and responsible information handling.
